Q1 Capacitance Definition and Energy
[6 marks]

(a) Define the capacitance of a capacitor.

(b) A capacitor has a charge of 200 μC on one plate when the potential difference across it is 50 V.

(i) Calculate the capacitance of the capacitor.

(ii) Calculate the energy stored in the capacitor.

(c) Explain why a capacitor stores energy but not charge.

Mark Scheme
Mark Distribution: C = correct answer only, A = any order, B = independent, M = method

(a) [2 marks]

C1Capacitance is defined as the ratio of charge stored to the potential difference across the plates: \(C = \frac{Q}{V}\)
C1Unit: Farad (F)

(b)(i) [2 marks]

C1\(C = \frac{Q}{V} = \frac{200 \times 10^{-6}}{50} = 4.0 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{F} = 4.0 \, \mu\text{F}\)

(b)(ii) [2 marks]

C1\(E = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{1}{2} \times 4.0 \times 10^{-6} \times 50^2\)
C1\(= 5.0 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{J} = 5.0 \, \text{mJ}\)

(c) [2 marks]

B1Capacitor has equal magnitudes of positive and negative charges (total charge = zero)
B1Work is done to separate the positive and negative charges — this work is stored as electrical potential energy
Q2 Parallel Plate Capacitor
[6 marks]

(a) State the formula for the capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor in terms of its physical dimensions.

(b) A parallel plate capacitor has plates of area 0.020 m² separated by a distance of 0.50 mm, with air between the plates.

(i) Calculate the capacitance. (ε₀ = 8.85 × 10⁻¹² F m⁻¹)

(ii) The capacitor is charged to a potential difference of 100 V. Calculate the charge on each plate.

(iii) Calculate the energy stored in the charged capacitor.

Mark Scheme

(a) [1 mark]

C1\(C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}\)

(b)(i) [2 marks]

C1\(C = \frac{8.85 \times 10^{-12} \times 0.020}{0.50 \times 10^{-3}}\)
C1\(= 3.54 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{F} = 354 \, \text{pF}\)

(b)(ii) [2 marks]

C1\(Q = CV = 3.54 \times 10^{-10} \times 100\)
C1\(= 3.54 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{C}\)

(b)(iii) [1 mark]

C1\(E = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{1}{2} \times 3.54 \times 10^{-10} \times 100^2\)
C1\(= 1.77 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{J}\)
Q3 Capacitors in Parallel
[4 marks]

(a) Two capacitors of capacitances 100 μF and 200 μF are connected in parallel across a battery of e.m.f. 9.0 V.

(i) Draw a circuit diagram showing this arrangement.

(ii) Calculate the total capacitance.

(iii) Calculate the total charge stored.

Mark Scheme

(a)(ii) [1 mark]

C1\(C_{total} = 100 + 200 = 300 \, \mu\text{F}\)

(a)(iii) [2 marks]

C1\(Q_{total} = C_{total}V = 300 \times 10^{-6} \times 9.0\)
C1\(= 2.7 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{C} = 2.7 \, \text{mC}\)
Q4 Capacitors in Series
[6 marks]

(a) Two capacitors of capacitance C₁ and C₂ are connected in series with a battery of e.m.f. V.

(i) State and explain what is the same about each capacitor.

(ii) Derive an expression for the total capacitance in terms of C₁ and C₂.

(b) Two 100 μF capacitors are connected in series with a 12 V battery. Calculate the total capacitance and the charge on each capacitor.

Mark Scheme

(a)(i) [2 marks]

B1Capacitors in series all store the same charge
B1Explanation: When charge +Q enters one plate, an equal charge −Q is repelled off the adjacent plate

(a)(ii) [2 marks]

M1\(V = V_1 + V_2 = \frac{Q}{C_1} + \frac{Q}{C_2} = Q(\frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2})\)
C1\(C_{total} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2}} = \frac{C_1 C_2}{C_1 + C_2}\)

(b) [2 marks]

C1\(C_{total} = \frac{100 \times 100}{100 + 100} = 50 \, \mu\text{F}\)
C1\(Q_1 = Q_2 = Q = C_{total}V = 50 \times 10^{-6} \times 12 = 6.0 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{C}\)
Q5 Charge and Energy Sharing
[8 marks]

(a) A 100 μF capacitor is charged to a potential difference of 20 V and then disconnected from the battery. It is connected across an identical uncharged capacitor.

(i) Calculate the initial charge on the first capacitor.

(ii) Calculate the total energy initially stored.

(iii) Calculate the final potential difference across each capacitor.

(iv) Calculate the total energy finally stored.

(v) Hence, calculate the energy lost during the sharing process.

(b) Explain why this energy is lost.

Mark Scheme

(a)(i) [1 mark]

C1\(Q = CV = 100 \times 10^{-6} \times 20 = 2.0 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{C} = 2.0 \, \text{mC}\)

(a)(ii) [1 mark]

C1\(E_i = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{1}{2} \times 100 \times 10^{-6} \times 20^2 = 2.0 \times 10^{-2} \, \text{J} = 20 \, \text{mJ}\)

(a)(iii) [2 marks]

M1Total capacitance: \(C_{total} = 100 + 100 = 200 \, \mu\text{F}\)
C1Conservation: \(Q_0 = Q_1 + Q_2 \Rightarrow V_f = \frac{Q_0}{C_{total}} = 10 \, \text{V}\)

(a)(iv) [2 marks]

C1\(E_f = \frac{1}{2}C_1 V_f^2 + \frac{1}{2}C_2 V_f^2 = 10 \, \text{mJ}\)

(a)(v) [1 mark]

C1\(\Delta E = E_i - E_f = 20 - 10 = 10 \, \text{mJ}\) (half the energy is lost)

(b) [1 mark]

B1Energy is lost because charge flowing through the connecting wires encounters resistance — electrical energy is dissipated as heat
Q6 Discharging a Capacitor
[8 marks]

(a) Define the time constant of a discharging circuit.

(b) A 500 μF capacitor is discharged through a 600 Ω resistor.

(i) Calculate the time constant of the circuit.

(ii) Calculate the time taken for the charge to fall to 37% of its initial value.

(iii) Calculate the time taken for the charge to fall to 1% of its initial value.

(c) State two factors that determine the rate at which a capacitor discharges.

Mark Scheme

(a) [2 marks]

B1The time constant τ is the time taken for the charge/current to fall to 1/e (approximately 37%) of its initial value
B1τ = RC

(b)(i) [1 mark]

C1\(\tau = RC = 600 \times 500 \times 10^{-6} = 0.30 \, \text{s}\)

(b)(ii) [1 mark]

C1By definition, after one time constant, charge = Q₀/e. Time = τ = 0.30 s

(b)(iii) [2 marks]

C1After 5τ, charge ≈ Q₀/100 (less than 1%)
C1Time = \(5 \times 0.30 = 1.5 \, \text{s}\)

(c) [2 marks]

B1Resistance R (larger R → slower discharge)
B1Capacitance C (larger C → slower discharge)
Q7 Exponential Discharge Equations
[6 marks]

(a) Write down the equations for:

(i) charge Q on a discharging capacitor at time t

(ii) potential difference V across the plates at time t

(iii) current I in the circuit at time t

In each case, state what each symbol represents.

(b) A 400 μF capacitor is charged to 20 V and then discharged through a resistor. The time constant of the circuit is 5.5 s.

(i) Write down the value of the resistance.

(ii) Calculate the potential difference across the capacitor 5.5 s after discharge begins.

Mark Scheme

(a)(i-iii) [3 marks]

C1\(Q = Q_0 e^{-t/RC}\) where Q₀ is initial charge, RC is time constant
C1\(V = V_0 e^{-t/RC}\) where V₀ is initial p.d.
C1\(I = I_0 e^{-t/RC}\) where I₀ is initial current

(b)(i) [1 mark]

C1\(R = \frac{\tau}{C} = \frac{5.5}{400 \times 10^{-6}} = 13750 \, \Omega = 13.75 \, k\Omega\)

(b)(ii) [2 marks]

C1After one time constant, \(V = V_0/e = V_0 \times 0.368\)
C1\(V = 20 \times 0.368 = 7.36 \, \text{V}\)
Q8 Camera Flash Application
[6 marks]

(a) In a simple photographic flashgun, a 0.20 F capacitor is charged by a 9.0 V battery.

(i) Calculate the energy stored in the fully charged capacitor.

(ii) The flash lasts for 0.010 s. Calculate the average power dissipated during the flash.

(iii) Calculate the approximate resistance of the bulb.

(b) State two advantages of using a capacitor in a camera flash rather than directly using the battery.

Mark Scheme

(a)(i) [1 mark]

C1\(E = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{1}{2} \times 0.20 \times 9.0^2 = 8.1 \, \text{J}\)

(a)(ii) [1 mark]

C1\(P = \frac{E}{t} = \frac{8.1}{0.010} = 810 \, \text{W}\)

(a)(iii) [1 mark]

C1Using \(P = V^2/R\): \(R = \frac{V^2}{P} = \frac{81}{810} = 0.10 \, \Omega\)

(b) [2 marks]

B1Capacitor can release energy much faster (higher power) than battery
B1Battery would be damaged by the high instantaneous current
Q9 Capacitor Network
[8 marks]

Three capacitors of capacitances 100 pF, 200 pF, and 600 pF are available.

(a) Calculate the maximum total capacitance that can be obtained by connecting them in any combination.

(b) Calculate the minimum total capacitance that can be obtained by connecting them in any combination.

(c) Sketch the circuits used in (a) and (b), labeling each capacitor with its capacitance value.

Mark Scheme

(a) [2 marks]

C1Maximum capacitance: all in parallel
C1\(C_{max} = 100 + 200 + 600 = 900 \, \text{pF}\)

(b) [2 marks]

C1Minimum capacitance: all in series
C1\(\frac{1}{C_{min}} = \frac{1}{100} + \frac{1}{200} + \frac{1}{600} = \frac{10}{600} \Rightarrow C_{min} = 60 \, \text{pF}\)
Q10 Discharge Graph Analysis
[6 marks]

A capacitor of capacitance 500 μF is discharged through a resistor of resistance R. The graph shows the variation of ln(Q) with time t.

(a) Use the graph to determine the time constant of the circuit.

(b) Hence, calculate the resistance R.

(c) Use the graph to find the initial charge Q₀ on the capacitor.

(d) Calculate the energy stored in the capacitor at t = 0.

Mark Scheme

(a) [2 marks]

M1Gradient of ln(Q) vs t graph = \(-1/\tau\)
C1From graph: gradient = \(-0.20 \, \text{s}^{-1}\) → \(\tau = \frac{1}{0.20} = 5.0 \, \text{s}\)

(b) [1 mark]

C1\(R = \frac{\tau}{C} = \frac{5.0}{500 \times 10^{-6}} = 10000 \, \Omega = 10 \, k\Omega\)

(c) [1 mark]

C1At t = 0, ln(Q) = ln(Q₀) = value from graph (e.g., if intercept = 13.8, then Q₀ = e¹³·⁸)

(d) [2 marks]

M1\(E = \frac{1}{2}CV_0^2\) or \(E = \frac{Q_0^2}{2C}\)
C1Calculate using values from graph
Exam Tips:
  • Watch units! Convert μF, nF to Farads; kΩ to Ohms
  • Series capacitors combine reciprocally — smaller than smallest
  • Parallel capacitors add — larger than largest
  • Energy sharing always loses half the energy
  • τ = RC has units of seconds (Ω × F = s)
