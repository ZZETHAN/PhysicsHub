A small sphere of mass \(m\) is suspended from a fixed point by a string of length \(L\). The sphere moves with constant speed \(v\) in a horizontal circle of radius \(r\), with the string making a constant angle \(\theta\) with the vertical.

(a) On the diagram, draw and label the forces acting on the sphere. [2]

(b) Show that the period \(T\) of the rotation is given by: \[T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L\cos\theta}{g}}\] where \(g\) is the acceleration of free fall. [4]

(c) The sphere completes 20 revolutions in 16 seconds. The length of the string is 1.2 m. Calculate the angle \(\theta\). [2]

Mark Scheme Click to show
Mark Types: B = Bookwork | M = Method | A = Answer
Part (a) - Force Diagram [2 marks]
B1
Tension correctly labelled along string towards suspension point
B1
Weight correctly labelled vertically downwards
Part (b) - Derivation [4 marks]
B1
Vertical resolution: \(T\cos\theta = mg\)
B1
Horizontal resolution: \(T\sin\theta = mr\omega^2\) or equivalent
B1
Elimination of \(T\) to obtain \(\tan\theta = \dfrac{r\omega^2}{g}\) or equivalent
B1
Use of \(r = L\sin\theta\) and \(\omega = \dfrac{2\pi}{T}\) with correct algebra leading to final expression
Part (c) - Calculation [2 marks]
B1
Correct substitution: \(T = \dfrac{16}{20} = 0.80\) s and \(L = 1.2\) m
B1
Correct answer: \(\theta = 50°\) (accept 48°–52°)
Examiner Tip: In conical pendulum problems, always resolve forces vertically (equilibrium) and horizontally (centripetal). The most common error is using the string length \(L\) instead of the radius \(r = L\sin\theta\) in the centripetal force equation. Remember: the radius of the circular path is the horizontal distance from the vertical axis, not the length of the string.

CIE 9702/42/O/N/2022
Vehicle on Banked Curve
[6 marks]

A car travels at speed \(v\) around a curve of radius \(r\) banked at an angle \(\theta\) to the horizontal. For a particular design of road, there is no sideways friction force on the car when it travels at the design speed.

(a) Explain why there is no sideways friction force at the design speed. [2]

(b) Show that the design speed is given by: \[v = \sqrt{gr\tan\theta}\] where \(g\) is the acceleration of free fall. [4]

Mark Scheme Click to show
Mark Types: B = Bookwork | M = Method | A = Answer
Part (a) - Explanation [2 marks]
B1
The horizontal component of the normal reaction provides the centripetal force OR the resultant of normal reaction and weight is horizontal (towards centre)
B1
No other horizontal force is needed / no component of forces parallel to the surface
Part (b) - Derivation [4 marks]
B1
Vertical resolution: \(R\cos\theta = mg\)
B1
Horizontal resolution: \(R\sin\theta = \dfrac{mv^2}{r}\)
B1
Division to eliminate \(R\): \(\tan\theta = \dfrac{v^2}{gr}\)
B1
Rearrangement to give required expression: \(v = \sqrt{gr\tan\theta}\)
Examiner Tip: For banked curves without friction, the normal reaction is the only force with a horizontal component. Always draw a clear force diagram showing the normal reaction perpendicular to the surface. Remember: the vertical component of normal reaction balances the weight, and the horizontal component provides the centripetal force.

CIE 9702/42/M/J/2022
Vertical Circular Motion
[7 marks]

A bucket of water is attached to a string and rotated in a vertical circle of radius \(r\).

(i) State the condition for the water to remain in the bucket when it is at the highest point of the circle. [1]

(a)(ii) [2 marks] Hence show that the minimum speed \(v_{min}\) at the highest point is given by: \[v_{min} = \sqrt{gr}\]

(b) The bucket is rotated with a constant angular velocity \(\omega\). The mass of water is \(m\). Derive an expression, in terms of \(m\), \(r\), \(\omega\), and \(g\), for the tension \(T\) in the string when the bucket is at the lowest point of the circle. [3]

(c) Explain why the tension in the string varies as the bucket moves around the circle, even though the angular velocity is constant. [1]

Mark Scheme Click to show
Mark Types: B = Bookwork | M = Method | A = Answer
Part (a)(i) - Condition [1 mark]
B1
The contact force/normal reaction on water (from bucket) must be greater than or equal to zero OR water must not lose contact with bucket
Part (a)(ii) - Derivation [2 marks]
B1
At minimum speed: \(mg = \dfrac{mv_{min}^2}{r}\) (tension/contact force = 0)
B1
Cancelling \(m\) and rearranging to give \(v_{min} = \sqrt{gr}\)
Part (b) - Derivation [3 marks]
B1
Force equation at lowest point: \(T - mg = mr\omega^2\) (or with \(v\))
B1
Correct direction: \(T\) upwards, \(mg\) downwards, resultant towards centre
B1
Correct expression: \(T = mr\omega^2 + mg\) or \(T = \dfrac{mv^2}{r} + mg\)
Part (c) - Explanation [1 mark]
B1
The weight of the water has a component towards/tangential to the centre which varies with position OR the resultant force required varies with position (as direction of weight relative to centre changes)
Examiner Tip: At the highest point of vertical circular motion, the critical condition is when the normal reaction becomes zero. Students often forget that at the lowest point, tension must overcome weight AND provide centripetal force, making it the point of maximum tension. Remember to always identify which forces point towards the centre at each position.
