Q3 RMS Values
[4 marks]

(a) Define the r.m.s. value of an alternating current. [2]

(b) The mains voltage is 230 V AC.

(i) Calculate the peak voltage. [1]

(ii) State the relationship between peak and r.m.s. values for sinusoidal AC. [1]

Mark Scheme

(a) r.m.s. = DC value producing same heating effect 2

(b)(i) $V_0 = V_{r.m.s.} \times \sqrt{2} = 325$ V 1

(ii) $V_{r.m.s.} = V_0/\sqrt{2}$ 1

Q4 Power in AC
[4 marks]

(a) An alternating current of peak value 10 A flows through a 5 Ω resistor.

Calculate the average power dissipated. [3]

(b) State one advantage of stating voltages as r.m.s. values rather than peak values. [1]

Mark Scheme

(a) $P_{avg} = I_{r.m.s.}^2 R = (I_0/\sqrt{2})^2 \times R = I_0^2 R / 2 = (10^2 \times 5)/2 = 250$ W 3

(b) Allows direct comparison with DC power calculations 1
