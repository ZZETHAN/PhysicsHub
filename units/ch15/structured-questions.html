Ideal Gases - Past Exam Questions with Mark Schemes

P4 Style Ideal Gas Equation
[8 marks]

(a) A container of volume 0.25 m³ contains an ideal gas at pressure 1.2 × 10⁵ Pa and temperature 300 K. [3]

(i) Calculate the number of moles of gas in the container.

(ii) Calculate the number of molecules in the container.

(b) The temperature of the gas is increased to 350 K at constant volume. [3]

(i) Calculate the new pressure of the gas.

(ii) Explain, in terms of molecular motion, why the pressure increases.

(c) State two assumptions of the kinetic theory of ideal gases. [2]

Mark Scheme
Mark Types: B = Bookwork | M = Method | A = Answer
(a)(i) - Moles calculation [1 mark]
M1
\(n = \frac{pV}{RT} = \frac{1.2 \times 10^5 \times 0.25}{8.31 \times 300}\)
A1
12.0 mol (accept 12 mol)
(ii) - Molecules calculation [1 mark]
M1
\(N = nN_A = 12.0 \times 6.02 \times 10^{23}\)
A1
\(7.2 \times 10^{24}\) molecules (accept 7.22 or 7.3)
(b)(i) - New pressure [1 mark]
M1
\(p_2 = p_1 \times \frac{T_2}{T_1} = 1.2 \times 10^5 \times \frac{350}{300}\)
A1
\(1.40 \times 10^5\) Pa (accept 1.4 × 10⁵)
(ii) - Explanation [2 marks]
B1
Higher temperature means molecules move faster / have greater kinetic energy
B1
More frequent collisions with walls / greater momentum change per collision
(c) - Assumptions [2 marks]
B1
Any two from: molecules in random motion; elastic collisions; negligible molecular volume; no intermolecular forces; large number of molecules
B1
Second valid assumption
Examiner Tip: Remember that temperature must be in Kelvin for all gas law calculations. For explanations about pressure, always link to molecular collisions with container walls.
P4 Style RMS Speed
[7 marks]

(a) State the equation relating the root-mean-square speed \(c_{rms}\) of gas molecules to the absolute temperature \(T\). [1]

(b) A gas cylinder contains helium gas (molar mass 4.0 g mol⁻¹) at a temperature of 300 K. [3]

(i) Calculate the root-mean-square speed of the helium atoms.

(ii) Calculate the average kinetic energy of a helium atom.

(c) The temperature of the gas is increased to 400 K. [3]

(i) Determine the ratio \(\frac{c_{rms}(400K)}{c_{rms}(300K)}\).

(ii) Explain why the average kinetic energy of the helium atoms increases.

Mark Scheme
Mark Types: B = Bookwork | M = Method | A = Answer
(a) - Equation [1 mark]
B1
\(c_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3kT}{m}}\) or \(c_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}}\)
(b)(i) - RMS speed calculation [2 marks]
M1
Correct substitution: \(c_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3 \times 8.31 \times 300}{0.004}}\)
A1
1370 m s⁻¹ (accept 1360-1380, allow 1.37 × 10³)
(ii) - Average kinetic energy [1 mark]
M1
\(E_k = \frac{3}{2}kT = \frac{3}{2} \times 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \times 300\)
A1
\(6.21 \times 10^{-21}\) J (accept 6.2 × 10⁻²¹)
(c)(i) - Ratio [1 mark]
M1
\(\frac{c_{400}}{c_{300}} = \sqrt{\frac{400}{300}} = \sqrt{\frac{4}{3}}\)
A1
1.15 (accept 1.154 or 2/√3)
(ii) - Explanation [2 marks]
B1
\(\langle E_k \rangle = \frac{3}{2}kT\), so kinetic energy is proportional to temperature
B1
Higher temperature means molecules move faster / have greater average kinetic energy
Examiner Tip: Remember that RMS speed depends on √(T) not T. For the same temperature, lighter molecules have higher RMS speed. Also note that average kinetic energy is the same for all gases at the same temperature!
💡 Exam Tips:
  • Always convert molar mass to kg mol⁻¹ for calculations (divide by 1000)
  • Remember: \(c_{rms} \propto \sqrt{T}\) and \(c_{rms} \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{m}}\)
  • Average kinetic energy \(\langle E_k \rangle = \frac{3}{2}kT\) depends only on temperature
  • At the same temperature, all ideal gas molecules have the same average kinetic energy
