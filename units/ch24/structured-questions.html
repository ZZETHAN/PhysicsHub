Piezoelectric Effect
CAIE P4 Ultrasound 4 marks
(a) State what is meant by the piezoelectric effect. [2]
(b) Explain why the optimum thickness of a piezoelectric crystal is \(\lambda/2\). [2]
(a) Definition
B1
Some crystals produce a potential difference when mechanically stressed.
B1
The reverse effect also occurs: an applied alternating pd makes the crystal vibrate.
(b) Resonance
B1
The crystal resonates when its thickness matches half a wavelength.
B1
Resonance gives maximum vibration amplitude, so ultrasound output is strongest.
2
Ultrasound Production
CAIE P4Ultrasound4 marks
(a) Describe how a piezoelectric transducer produces ultrasound pulses. [2]
(b) Why must the natural frequency match the target ultrasound frequency? [2]
B1
An alternating pd causes the crystal to contract and expand repeatedly.
B1
This produces ultrasound pulses; returning echoes can also make the crystal generate an electrical signal.
B1
Matching frequencies gives resonance.
B1
That makes both emission and detection more efficient.

Acoustic Impedance
CAIE P4Ultrasound5 marks
(a) Define acoustic impedance \(Z\). [1]
(b) Soft tissue has density \(1060\,\mathrm{kg\,m^{-3}}\) and sound speed \(1540\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\). Calculate \(Z\). [2]
(c) What happens when the impedance mismatch at a boundary is large? [2]
A1
\(Z = \rho c\).
M1
Substitute \(1060 \times 1540\).
A1
\(1.63 \times 10^6\,\mathrm{kg\,m^{-2}\,s^{-1}}\) or Rayl.
B1
A large fraction of the ultrasound is reflected.
B1
So little energy is transmitted across the boundary.
4
Coupling Gel
CAIE P4Ultrasound4 marks
(a) Why is coupling gel essential in ultrasound imaging? [2]
(b) What would happen without gel? Explain using acoustic impedance. [2]
B1
The gel removes the air gap between probe and skin.
B1
That reduces the impedance mismatch and lets ultrasound enter the body.
B1
Air and tissue have very different acoustic impedances.
B1
So most of the ultrasound would be reflected at the skin surface.
5
A-scan
CAIE P4Ultrasound6 marks
(a) How does an A-scan determine the depth of a boundary? [2]
(b) A pulse takes 0.15 ms to return in soft tissue where \(c = 1540\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\). Find the depth. [3]
(c) Why must the scan be pulsed rather than continuous? [1]
B1
Measure the time between sending a pulse and receiving its echo.
B1
Use depth = \(c\Delta t / 2\) because the pulse travels to the boundary and back.
M1
\(\text{depth} = 1540 \times 0.15 \times 10^{-3} / 2\)
A1
\(0.1155\,\mathrm{m}\)
A1
\(11.6\,\mathrm{cm}\) (accept 11.5 cm).
B1
The transducer cannot emit and detect echoes at the same time.
6
B-scan
CAIE P4Ultrasound4 marks
(a) Distinguish between A-scan and B-scan output. [2]
(b) State one advantage and one limitation of B-scan imaging. [2]
B1
A-scan shows echo amplitude against time (or depth) in one dimension.
B1
B-scan uses many A-scans to form a two-dimensional brightness image.
B1
Advantage: shows the position and shape of internal structures.
B1
Limitation: image quality can be reduced by movement or poor penetration.
7
Ultrasound Frequency
CAIE P4Ultrasound4 marks
(a) Higher frequency ultrasound gives better resolution. Explain why. [2]
(b) Why does higher frequency ultrasound have shallower penetration? [2]
B1
Higher frequency means shorter wavelength.
B1
Shorter wavelengths can resolve smaller details.
B1
Higher frequency waves are attenuated more strongly.
B1
So their intensity falls more quickly with depth.
8
X-ray Production
CAIE P4X-rays4 marks
(a) Describe the structure of an X-ray tube and explain how X-rays are produced. [2]
(b) Distinguish bremsstrahlung from characteristic X-rays. [2]
B1
The cathode emits electrons which are accelerated by a high potential difference towards a tungsten anode.
B1
Rapid deceleration and inner-shell transitions in the target produce X-rays.
B1
Bremsstrahlung gives a continuous spectrum from electron deceleration.
B1
Characteristic X-rays are discrete energies from electron transitions between shells.

X-ray Attenuation
CAIE P4X-rays6 marks
(a) State the Beer-Lambert law for X-ray attenuation. [2]
(b) The initial intensity is \(100\,\mathrm{mW\,cm^{-2}}\), \(\mu = 0.35\,\mathrm{cm^{-1}}\), and thickness \(x = 3.0\,\mathrm{cm}\). Find the transmitted intensity. [3]
(c) Why does bone appear white on an X-ray image? [1]
B1
\(I = I_0 e^{-\mu x}\)
B1
Intensity decreases exponentially with thickness for a parallel beam.
M1
\(I = 100e^{-0.35 \times 3.0}\)
A1
\(I \approx 35\,\mathrm{mW\,cm^{-2}}\)
A1
Correct unit or suitably rounded numerical answer.
B1
Bone contains high-Z materials such as calcium, so it absorbs X-rays more strongly.

CT Scanning
CAIE P4X-rays5 marks
(a) How does CT produce cross-sectional images? [2]
(b) State one advantage of CT compared with plain X-ray imaging. [1]
(c) Why does CT require fast computers and large memory? [2]
B1
The X-ray source and detectors rotate to collect many projections.
B1
A computer reconstructs a slice from all the attenuation data.
B1
CT shows cross-sections without superposition of structures.
B1
Large amounts of detector data must be processed quickly.
B1
Reconstruction involves many repeated calculations and image storage.
11
PET Process
CAIE P4PET6 marks
(a) Describe the emission process used in PET. [3]
(b) State the energy and relative directions of the emitted photons. [2]
(c) Why must PET tracers have short half-lives? [1]
B1
A radioactive nucleus emits a positron in beta-plus decay.
B1
The positron travels a short distance before annihilating with an electron.
B1
Annihilation produces two gamma photons.
B1
Each photon has energy 511 keV.
B1
They are emitted in opposite directions, about 180° apart.
B1
Short half-life reduces patient dose while still allowing imaging soon after injection.
12
PET Detection
CAIE P4PET5 marks
(a) How does coincidence detection locate the source in PET? [3]
(b) What is a line of response (LOR)? [2]
B1
Two detectors on opposite sides detect photons at the same time.
B1
This coincidence identifies a possible line through the source.
B1
Many coincidence events are combined by a computer to reconstruct the tracer distribution.
B1
The LOR is the straight line joining the two detectors that fire simultaneously.
B1
The annihilation event occurred somewhere along that line.
