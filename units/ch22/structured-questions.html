Q1 Photon Energy
[4 marks]

(a) State the formula for photon energy in terms of frequency. [1]

(b) Calculate the energy of a photon with wavelength 550 nm. [3]

Given: \(E = hc/\lambda\), \(h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34}\) J·s, \(c = 3.00 \times 10^8\) m/s

Mark Scheme
Mark Distribution: C = correct answer only, A = any order, B = independent, M = method

(a) [1 mark]

C1E = hf (or E = hc/λ)

(b) [3 marks]

C1E = hc/λ
M1= (6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ × 3.00 × 10⁸) / (550 × 10⁻⁹)
A1= 3.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J
Q2 Photoelectric Effect
[6 marks]

(a) Define threshold frequency. [2]

(b) State two observations from the photoelectric effect that wave theory cannot explain. [2]

(c) Light of frequency \(6.0 \times 10^{14}\) Hz hits a metal with work function Φ = 2.4 eV. Calculate whether electrons are emitted. [2]

Mark Scheme

(a) [2 marks]

B1Minimum frequency of incident radiation for electron emission from metal surface
B1Below this frequency, NO electrons are emitted regardless of intensity

(b) [2 marks]

B1Existence of threshold frequency (no emission below f₀)
B1Instantaneous emission (no time lag even at low intensity)

(c) [2 marks]

C1Photon energy E = hf = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ × 6.0 × 10¹⁴
M1= 3.98 × 10⁻¹⁹ J = 2.49 eV
A1Since E > Φ (2.49 eV > 2.4 eV), electrons ARE emitted
Q3 Einstein's Equation
[6 marks]

(a) Write \(hf = \Phi + KE_{\max}\) and define each symbol. [2]

(b) Light of wavelength 450 nm hits a metal with work function Φ = 2.3 eV. Calculate the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons (in eV). [3]

(c) State the effect of increasing the light intensity on \(KE_{\max}\). [1]

Mark Scheme

(a) [2 marks]

B1hf = photon energy; Φ = work function (minimum energy to escape)
B1KEₘₐₓ = maximum kinetic energy of emitted electron

(b) [3 marks]

C1E = hf = hc/λ = (1240 eV·nm) / 450 nm = 2.76 eV
M1KEₘₐₓ = hf − Φ = 2.76 − 2.3
A1= 0.46 eV

(c) [1 mark]

B1No effect on KEₘₐₓ (depends only on frequency, not intensity)

Q5 de Broglie Wavelength
[5 marks]

(a) State de Broglie's hypothesis. [2]

(b) An electron is accelerated through a potential difference of 150 V. Calculate its de Broglie wavelength. [3]

Given: \(m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\) kg, \(e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19}\) C, \(h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34}\) J·s

Mark Scheme

(a) [2 marks]

B1All matter has wave-like properties (wave-particle duality)
B1Wavelength λ = h/p = h/(mv)

(b) [3 marks]

C1K.E. = eV = 150 eV (convert to joules: 150 × 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)
C1λ = h/√(2mₑeV) for accelerated electron
M1= (6.63 × 10⁻³⁴) / √(2 × 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ × 150 × 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)
A1= 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁰ m (0.10 nm)

Q7 Energy Levels
[6 marks]

(a) In an energy level diagram, state which level is the ground state. [1]

(b) An electron falls from energy level \(E_2 = -3.4\) eV to \(E_1 = -13.6\) eV. Calculate the energy of the emitted photon (in eV). [2]

(c) A photon with energy greater than the ionization energy is absorbed. What happens to the electron? [1]

Mark Scheme

(a) [1 mark]

B1E₀ (lowest energy level, n=1)

(b) [2 marks]

C1ΔE = E(final) − E(initial) = E₁ − E₂
A1ΔE = (−13.6) − (−3.4) = 10.2 eV

(c) [1 mark]

B1Electron becomes free / atom is ionized (electron ejected from atom)
