Thermodynamics - Past Exam Questions with Mark Schemes

P4 Style First Law
[8 marks]

(a) A gas is heated by supplying it with 250 kJ of thermal energy; at the same time, it is compressed so that 500 kJ of work is done on the gas. [2]

Calculate the change in the internal energy of the gas.

(b) The same gas is heated as before with 250 kJ of energy. This time the gas is allowed to expand so that it does 200 kJ of work on its surroundings. [2]

Calculate the change in the internal energy of the gas.

(c) Using the First Law of Thermodynamics, explain what happens to the temperature of the gas in each case. [4]

Mark Scheme
Mark Types: B = Bookwork | M = Method | A = Answer
(a) - Internal energy change [2 marks]
M1
Correct application of First Law: ΔU = Q + W = 250 + 500
A1
750 kJ (accept positive sign)
(b) - Internal energy change [2 marks]
M1
Correct application: ΔU = Q - W = 250 - 200
A1
50 kJ
(c) - Temperature explanation [4 marks]
B1
For case (a): internal energy increases (so temperature increases)
B1
For case (b): internal energy increases (so temperature increases)
B1
For ideal gas: U ∝ T, so ΔU positive means temperature increases
B1
Temperature increase is greater in (a) because both heating and compression add to internal energy
Examiner Tip: Remember the sign convention! Work done ON the gas is positive (compression), work done BY the gas is positive (expansion) when using ΔU = Q - W. For ideal gases, internal energy is directly proportional to absolute temperature.
P4 Style P-V Diagram
[12 marks]
[P-V Diagram: State A to B - insert from past paper]

An ideal gas undergoes a change from state A to state B as shown on the P-V diagram above. The pressure and volume at the two states are:

State A: PA = 2.0 × 10⁵ Pa, VA = 1.0 × 10⁻³ m³
State B: PB = 1.0 × 10⁵ Pa, VB = 3.0 × 10⁻³ m³

(a) In the process of change from state A to state B, is the gas doing work on the surroundings or are the surroundings doing work on the gas? Explain your answer. [2]

(b) Is the work done on the gas positive or negative? What is the magnitude of the work? [3]

(c) Calculate the work done by the gas during this process. You may assume that the average pressure during the expansion is (PA + PB)/2. [4]

(d) If the internal energy of the gas at state A is 450 J and at state B is 600 J, calculate the heat transferred during the process and state whether it is absorbed or released. [3]

Mark Scheme
Mark Types: B = Bookwork | M = Method | A = Answer
(a) - Work direction [2 marks]
B1
Gas does work on surroundings (or work done by gas)
B1
Volume increases / gas expands / pushes piston out
(b) - Sign and magnitude [3 marks]
B1
Work done on gas is negative
B1
Magnitude equals area under curve
B1
Acceptable range given for calculation
(c) - Work calculation [4 marks]
M1
Average pressure: Pavg = (2.0 + 1.0) × 10⁵ / 2 = 1.5 × 10⁵ Pa
M1
Volume change: ΔV = (3.0 - 1.0) × 10⁻³ = 2.0 × 10⁻³ m³
M1
Work formula: W = Pavg × ΔV
A1
300 J (accept 290-310 J)
(d) - Heat transfer [3 marks]
M1
ΔU = 600 - 450 = 150 J
M1
Q = ΔU + W = 150 + 300 = 450 J
A1
Heat is absorbed (by gas) / enters system
Examiner Tip: The area under a P-V curve represents work done. For expansion, work is done BY the gas (Wby positive, Won negative). Remember Q = ΔU + Wby or Q = ΔU - Won depending on your sign convention.
P4 Style Cyclic Process
[10 marks]
[P-V Diagram: Cycle A→B→C→A - insert from past paper]

An ideal gas undergoes a cyclic process A → B → C → A as shown on the P-V diagram. The process consists of:

  • A → B: Isobaric expansion at pressure P₁
  • B → C: Isochoric cooling at volume V₂
  • C → A: Compression back to the initial state

(a) State what is meant by a cyclic process and explain why the change in internal energy for a complete cycle is zero. [2]

(b) For the process B → C:

  • state the name of this type of process (1 mark)
  • calculate the work done during this process (1 mark)
  • explain how the internal energy of the gas changes (2 marks)

(c) If the internal energies at states A and C are equal, explain why the pressure at C must be lower than at B. [4]

Mark Scheme
Mark Types: B = Bookwork | M = Method | A = Answer
(a) - Cyclic process [2 marks]
B1
System returns to initial state / start and end at same state
B1
Internal energy is a state function / depends only on state / same state means same U
(b) - Process B→C [4 marks]
B1
Isochoric / isovolumetric / constant volume
A1
Zero (no work done)
B1
Temperature decreases (pressure decreases at constant volume)
B1
Internal energy decreases (U ∝ T for ideal gas)
(c) - Pressure explanation [4 marks]
B1
UA = UC means TA = TC (as U ∝ T)
B1
From B to C: volume is constant, pressure decreases
B1
Using pV = nRT: at constant V, p ∝ T
B1
As T decreases from B to C, p must decrease / pC < pB
Examiner Tip: For cyclic processes, ΔU = 0 for complete cycle, so Qnet = Wnet. The area enclosed by the cycle on a P-V diagram represents the net work done. Isochoric means constant volume (vertical line on P-V diagram), isobaric means constant pressure (horizontal line).
💡 Exam Tips:
  • Always use the correct sign convention for work and heat
  • Remember: work = area under P-V curve
  • For ideal gases, U ∝ T only (internal energy depends only on temperature)
  • In isochoric processes, W = 0 and Q = ΔU
  • In isothermal processes, ΔU = 0 and Q = W
  • In adiabatic processes, Q = 0 and ΔU = -W
